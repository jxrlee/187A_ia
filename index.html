Simplified Main Navigation Tally

Only labels used by two or more sites are tallied. Looking at the labels, we felt that everything could be broken down into four main label: about, admissions, academics, and campus life. The about would contain all the information visitors and prospective students would want to learn about the university. The admissions would contain all information on applying and the different programs. The academics contains information for current students such as academic calendar, courses, and links to their college. Lastly, any prospective student or parent might wonder about the campus life of the school. Here, people can find out about student organizations, sports, culture and living at UL.

Hardest Decisions

Home Page

Looking at various university sites, it was easy to see that there was no distinct design choice when it came to designing the home page. Some sites chose to use the home page merely as a portal into all the secondary pages. These portal home pages tend to be short and have all the graphics, links, and contents above the page fold. The University of Louisiana Lafayette home page is one example. Other university sites chose to incorporate and blend the home page as to make it like all of its secondary pages. These home pages tend to use the content section for school news and other announcements and typically require scrolling to reach the bottom of the page. We decided to go with something in between the two, which gave us the advantage of a minimalistic page while retaining its usefulness. Our home page would utilize the area above the fold to display news and announcements while we would also provide full site links for quick access underneath. This allowed people to quickly navigate to their destination rather than having to navigate through many levels before getting to information. This was the biggest problem we had with the current site and we hoped to fix it with our quick links.

Main Navigation Dropdown Menus

Deciding on our four main navigation labels was the easy part. The hard part was choosing which links to put under each one. We looked at each of the five university and the subchilds of each main navigation label to decide on our own submenus. A pet peeve of ours is when we hover or click and a huge dropdown list appears. We wanted to make sure that our dropdown menus only contained the most frequently used items and nothing that doesn’t need to be there. This keeps our site consistent with our minimalist theme and the dropdowns more visually appealing.
